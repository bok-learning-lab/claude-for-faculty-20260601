Assistant Director, International Teachers and Scholars

As Assistant Director, International Teachers and Scholars, Sarah coordinates the Derek Bok Center's Professional Communication Program for International Teachers and Scholars. With more than 15 years of experience working with multilingual scholars in the U.S., South Korea, and China, Sarah’s portfolio includes training and resources for those looking to develop their skills in oral English, intercultural communication, public speaking, or teaching. She provides workshops, seminars, and consultations to all PhD students as well as creates resources and online training materials for those looking for more flexible practice. Sarah collaborates with Harvard Griffin GSAS on supporting international scholars through providing English language assessment interviews and designing the academic and professional communication curriculum for the Griffin GSAS Welcomes: International Summer Institute. Sarah manages an undergraduate fellows team of Culture and Communication Consultants, undergraduate students who participate in and lead programming for international graduate students. Sarah particularly enjoys the opportunity to speak with and learn from scholars from across the university!

Sarah has received certificates in TEFL, TESOL, and as an ACTFL rater. She holds a Master of Arts in Humanities and Women’s Studies Certificate from Duke University and a Bachelor of Arts in English Literature and Religious Studies from the University of Pittsburgh.

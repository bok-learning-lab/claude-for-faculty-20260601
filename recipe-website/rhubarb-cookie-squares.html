Rhubarb Cookie Squares

11 × 7 × 2 pan, baked at 350°

About this recipe

Rhubarb is an old Asian medicinal plant — its dried root traveled the Silk Road for over a thousand years — but rhubarb as a dessert ingredient is comparatively recent. It only became affordable in Europe in the 19th century, once sugar prices dropped enough to tame its sourness, and it spread across North America as a hardy farm-garden plant that came up reliably in early spring before any other fruit was ready. That's why so many traditional rhubarb recipes — pies, crumbles, jams, and cookie bars like this one — come from the Midwest and the Plains: it was the first sweet thing of the year.

This is a classic two-layer bar cookie, a Midwestern church-and-potluck genre: a shortbread crust pressed into a pan, partially baked, then topped with a custardy fruit filling and finished in the oven. The "Higbee's" stamp on the card is a nice clue — Higbee's was a Cleveland, Ohio department store, beloved into the 1990s (and the toy department where Ralphie ogles the Red Ryder BB gun in A Christmas Story). So this is almost certainly an Ohio kitchen, working off a recipe card the store printed and gave away — a small piece of 20th-century American department-store cooking culture.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ⅓ cup confectioners' sugar
  • ⅓ cup butter or margarine

Filling

  • 1 cup sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 3 cups finely diced fresh or frozen rhubarb

Directions

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, powdered sugar, and butter to coarse crumbs.
  2. Press into the bottom of a greased pan. Bake at 350° for 12 minutes.
  3. For the filling, combine the first 4 ingredients (sugar, flour, eggs, vanilla).
  4. Stir in the rhubarb and pour over the warm crust.
  5. Bake 35–40 minutes more.
